Welcome to Adlam and N'ko Annotation

Annotation, in the context of language models, refers to the process of labeling text with specific tags or information. This information can include translations, grammatical tags, or any other metadata that helps in building and improving language models. The annotated data is used to train language models to better understand and generate text in different languages.

How Annotations are Used

Annotated data is crucial for training machine learning models. In the context of language models, annotations provide the necessary information to learn from. For example, translating French sentences into Adlam script or vice versa helps the model learn how to perform these translations accurately.

Natural Language Processing (NLP) Tasks

NLP tasks involve various processes that enable language models to understand and generate human language. Here are some key NLP tasks and examples of how they can be applied to Adlam and N'ko languages:

  • Translation: Translating text from one language to another.
  • Sentiment Analysis: Determining the sentiment or emotional tone of a piece of text.
  • Text Summarization: Generating a summary of a longer text.
  • Named Entity Recognition: Identifying and classifying entities (like names, dates, locations) in text.
  • Part-of-Speech Tagging: Labeling each word in a text with its grammatical role (noun, verb, adjective, etc.).

Once we develop robust AI models for Adlam and N'ko languages, we can leverage these models for various NLP tasks similar to those demonstrated above. Here are some exciting possibilities:

  • Sentiment Analysis: Understand the emotional tone in Adlam and N'ko texts, which can be useful for social media analysis, customer feedback, and more.
