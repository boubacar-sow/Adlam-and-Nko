About N'ko

The N'ko script was invented by Solomana Kanté in 1949 to write the Mandenkan languages, including Maninka, Bambara, and Dyula. The script is used primarily in Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Mali, Ivory Coast, and Burkina Faso. N'ko is written from right to left and has a standardized grammar known as "kángbɛ," which means "language-manner". The script has been successful in preserving and promoting the written form of these languages.
